I am Ashley Taylor

Post-Production Video Editing

My name is Ashley Taylor. I am 24 years young, and I love what I do as a video editor. As an editor, I get to visually aid in both telling and putting stories together. Some of my most recent edited works consist of a short called “Parties Collide”, a BTS for a student short film called “Halo Night”, and various dance pieces for the UVU Ballroom Company. I have always had a passion for storytelling, I grew up hearing stories from my mom about when she toured with her dance group in college, so at a young age, I aspired to do the same.

In 2018, I began taking classes at Utah Valley University pursuing Dance. I worked my way up to the Ballroom Backup Touring Team and danced with the company for several semesters. In the spring of 2021, I graduated with an associate’s in Dance. As a dancer, I told stories through movement and I absolutely loved it. Later, I realized that my love of storytelling stretched far beyond dancing. During my time as a dancer at UVU, I took a Dance and Technology course which introduced me to video editing. This changed the ambitions and aspirations I had for myself as a dancer and storyteller. I found that film offered me an opportunity to take my love of storytelling to another level.

I have since switched my bachelor’s program to Digital Cinema with a focus in Post-Production, to continue cultivating my editing talent. I have found that there are many parallels in my work as an editor to when I was a dancer. I am constantly drawing on my knowledge of movement in making my edits to give visual continuity. I take raw video files, like I would with simple and complex movement when choreographing, and then I refine it until it becomes a well told story that’s ready for debut. I am grateful for the journey and the experiences that continue to serve me as an aspiring video editor. I look forward to telling more stories through cinematic means.

Senior Project

As a former dancer for Utah Valley University, I would love to give back to the Dance Department through my senior project. My aspirations for my senior project would involve UVU dancers. Ideally, I would love to work with the different Dance Departments and showcase a piece or pieces choreographed specifically with the camera in mind, blending both the art of dance and art of cinema together. This would be a Dance for Film and I would have it filmed in such a way that it becomes more than just a simple stage spectacle. I plan to play an active part in pre-production and on set in addition to editing the pieces together in post. It takes a village to raise a child and it takes a cast and crew to craft a film. As I continue to work closely with fellow filmmakers in the program, I hope that when the time comes I can hand pick my crew to raise this idea from its infancy into a well-regarded cinematic dance masterpiece.
